Lotka-Volterra Model Theory

The Predator-Prey dynamics simulated here are based on the Lotka-Volterra equations, which describe how predator and prey populations change over time.

The model uses these equations:

Prey population change: dP/dt = αP - βP×Q

Predator population change: dQ/dt = δβP×Q - γQ

Where:

  • P = Prey population
  • Q = Predator population
  • α = Prey growth rate (controlled by slider)
  • β = Predation rate (controlled by slider)
  • γ = Predator death rate (controlled by slider)
  • δ = Conversion efficiency (controlled by slider)

The simulation shows the characteristic oscillations of predator and prey populations, where:

  1. Prey population increases → Predators have more food → Predator population increases
  2. Increased predators → More prey eaten → Prey population decreases
  3. Prey population decreases → Predators have less food → Predator population decreases
  4. Fewer predators → Less predation → Prey population increases

The cycle then repeats! Adjust the parameters to see how they affect the stability and dynamics of the system.
